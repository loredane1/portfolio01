How this set fits production. I deliver the dimensioned drawings and specifications — not the final cutting list. At the production stage, a fabrication checker validates the on-site measurements and defines the cutting standard for the shop. Having worked inside a cabinetry manufacturer, I sat with the carpenters to review each project and iron out the details before production began — so the drawings and the shop floor always spoke the same language.
Project Concept

Integration, storage and spatial clarity.

Designed for a compact integrated apartment where cabinetry acts as an architectural solution, not just furniture. A functional wall organizes circulation, creates kitchen storage and serves as the sofa panel opposite — keeping the apartment feeling open.

A light palette, punctual warm-oak woodgrain, black pulls and layered lighting balance function, visual comfort and perceptual order — the result is a space that reads as light, efficient and technically clear for cabinetry execution.

Project Objectives

Storage

Expand storage capacity without visually overloading the compact footprint.

Integration

Connect kitchen and living area through a dual-function millwork wall.

Clarity

Reduce visual noise with light materials, finish continuity and clean module reading.

Functionality

Organize prep, storage, circulation and social areas for practical daily use.

Comfort

Use task lighting in the kitchen and warmer lighting in the living area to encourage dwell time.

Production

Document every decision with legible sheets, millimeter dimensioning and objective specifications.
