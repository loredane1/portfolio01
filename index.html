Signature Project · Original Concept

A shared room that feels like home

Designed for two teenage sisters who spend alternate weekends at their father's, this small bedroom had to do more than fit two of everything. It had to feel like theirs.

It is not built on neuroarchitecture, but it borrows a few of its ideas where they earn their place, starting with indirect lighting built into the cabinetry to keep glare out.

  • Night-friendly light: a dedicated lamp lets one sister get up at night without waking the other.
  • Storage that works harder: the lower bunk is a storage bed and the steps double as drawers.
  • Rolling bedside table: on casters, there when it is needed and out of the way when it is not.
  • A deliberately short TV panel: kept clear of the AC unit, which drips and could damage the millwork.

Project Concept

Integration, storage and spatial clarity.

Cabinetry as an architectural solution, not just furniture. A functional wall organizes circulation, holds the kitchen storage and becomes the sofa panel on the other side, which keeps the apartment open.

A light palette, warm-oak accents, black pulls and layered light balance function, visual comfort and order. The space reads as light, efficient and technically clear to build.

Project Objectives

Storage

Add storage without visually overloading the compact footprint.

Integration

Connect kitchen and living through one dual-function millwork wall.

Clarity

Cut visual noise with light materials, finish continuity and clean module reading.

Functionality

Organize prep, storage, circulation and social areas for daily use.

Comfort

Task light in the kitchen, warmer light in the living area to invite people to stay.
